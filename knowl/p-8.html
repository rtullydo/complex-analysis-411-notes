Let \(f:A \to \C\) be analytic and \(f\dd(z_0) \neq 0\) for \(z_0 \in A\text{.}\) Then there exists a neighborhood \(U\) of \(z_0\) and a neighborhood \(V\) of \(f(z_0)\) so that
  1. the restriction \(f:U \to V\) is a bijection,
  2. \(f\inv:V \to U\) is analytic,
  3. and
    \begin{equation*} \frac{d}{dw} f\inv(w) = \frac{1}{f\dd(z)} \end{equation*}
    where \(w = f(z)\text{.}\)
