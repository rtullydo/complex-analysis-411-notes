Finally, we'll look an example application. In the study of heat, the harmonic function \(T\) on a domain represents temperature, and its level curves \(Tx, y) = c\) are called isotherms. The harmonic conjugate of \(T\text{,}\) which we can denote \(T\ad\) has level curves that represent the flux lines induced by the gradient field \(\nabla T\text{.}\) Our task is to find a harmonic function on the unit disk with the left half circle on the boundary held at temperature \(T = 0\) and the right circle held at \(T = 1\text{.}\)
