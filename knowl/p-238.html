Let \(f(t)\) be bounded and locally integrable for \(t \geq 0\text{,}\) and assume that

\begin{equation*} g(s) = \int_0^\infty e^{-st}f(t)\, dt \text{ with } \RE s > 0 \end{equation*}

extends to a holomorphic function on \(\RE s \geq 0\text{.}\) Then

\begin{equation*} \int_0^\infty f(t)\, dt \text{ converges} \end{equation*}

and equals \(g(0)\text{.}\)
