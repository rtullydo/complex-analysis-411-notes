Theorem 3.3.11. Ikehara-Weiner.

Let \(f(x)\) be nonnegative and nondecreasing on \([1,\infty)\) and such that the Mellin transform
\begin{equation*} g_0(s) = \int_1^\infty x^{-s} \, df(x) = -f(1) + s\int_1^s \frac{f(x)}{x^{s+1}}\,dx \end{equation*}
exists for \(\RE s > 1\text{.}\) Suppose that for some constant \(c\text{,}\) the function
\begin{equation*} g_0(s) - \frac{c}{s-1} \end{equation*}
has a continuous extension to the closed half-plane \(\RE s \geq 1\text{.}\) Then
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{f(x)}{x} = c. \end{equation*}
