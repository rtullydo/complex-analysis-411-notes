  1. (analytic) \(\zeta(s) = \sum_{n} \frac{1}{n^s}\) is an analytic function on \(\RE s \gt 1\text{.}\)
  2. (product formula)
    \begin{equation*} \zeta(s) = \prod_{p} (1 - p^{-s})\inv\text{.} \end{equation*}
  3. (zero-free region) \(\zeta\) has no zeros in \(\{s: \RE s > 1\}\text{.}\)
  4. (meromorphic continuation) \(\zeta\) has a unique meromorphic continuation to \(\C\) with a simple pole at \(s=1\) and no other poles. Moreover, after removing the pole, \((s-1)\zeta(s)\) is an entire function of order 1.
  5. (functional equation)
    \begin{equation*} \zeta(s) = 2^s \pi^{s-1} \sin \frac{\pi s}{2} \Gamma(1-s) \zeta(1-s). \end{equation*}
