Proof

We'll need the straightforward fact that

\begin{equation*} \abs{n^{s}} = n^{\RE s}. \end{equation*}

Let \(s\) be a complex number with \(\RE s > 1\text{.}\) There are many ways to construct the next part of the argument. We'll use the \(M\)-test. Since

\begin{equation*} \abs{\frac{1}{n^s}} \leq \frac{1}{n^{\RE s}}, \end{equation*}

and the terms \(\frac{1}{n^{\RE s}}\) form a convergent \(p\)-series as \(\RE s > 1\text{,}\) the Weierstrass \(M\)-test says that the series converges uniformly to \(\zeta\) where \(\RE s > 1\text{.}\) Each function \(f_n = \frac{1}{n^s}\) is analytic on \(\RE s > 1\) (given a suitable choice of branch), and thus by TheoremÂ 3.1.3, the limit function \(\zeta\) must also be holomorphic for \(\RE s > 1\text{.}\)
