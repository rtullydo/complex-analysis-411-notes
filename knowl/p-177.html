Let \(f_n\) be a sequence of holomorphic functions on a domain \(D\text{,}\) and assume that \(\sum_n f_n\) converges uniformly on every closed and bounded subset of \(D\text{.}\) Then

  1. \(f = \sum_n f_n\) is holomorphic on \(D\text{.}\)

  2. For any \(k \geq 1\text{,}\) the series of \(k\)th derivatives \(\sum_n f_n^{(k)}\) converges on \(D\) and converges uniformly and absolutely on every closed and bounded subset of \(D\) to the function \(f^{(k)}\text{.}\) That is, the derivative of the limit is the limit of the derivatives.
