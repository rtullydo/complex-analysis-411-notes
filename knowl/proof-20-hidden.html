First,

\begin{equation*} \th(x) = \sum_{p \leq x} \log p \leq \sum_{p \leq x} \log x = \pi(x) \log x. \end{equation*}

Thus,

\begin{equation*} \liminf_{x \to \infty} \frac{\pi(x) \log x}{x} \geq \liminf_{x \to \infty} \frac{\th(x)}{x} = 1. \end{equation*}

In the other direction, for \(0 \lt \eps \lt 1\text{,}\)

\begin{align*} \th(x) \amp\geq \sum_{x^{1 - \eps} \leq p \leq x} \log p\\ \amp\geq (1 - \eps) \sum_{x^{1 - \eps} \leq p \leq x} \log x\\ \amp= (1-\eps) \log x (\pi(x) + O(x^{1-\eps})). \end{align*}

Then for each \(\eps\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} \limsup_{x \to \infty} \frac{\pi(x) \log x}{x} \leq \frac{1}{1 - \eps} \limsup_{x \to \infty} \frac{\th(x)}{x}. \end{equation*}

We conclude that

\begin{equation*} \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{\pi(x) \log x}{x} = 1. \end{equation*}
