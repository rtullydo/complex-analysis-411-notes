Theorem 1.1.8.

Let \(A\) be a simply connected domain that isn't all of \(\C\text{.}\) Then there exists a bijective conformal map \(f: A \to \D\text{.}\) Futher, for any fixed \(z_0 \in A\text{,}\) we can find \(f\) so that \(f(z_0) = 0\) and \(f\dd(z_0) > 0\text{.}\) With these conditions, the map \(f\) is unique.
