Theorem 3.2.10. Fundamental theorem of arithmetic.

Every natural number \(n \in \mathbb N\) has a unique representation as a product
\begin{equation*} n = \prod_{p} p^{a_p}, \end{equation*}
where the product is over the primes \(p\) and the exponents \(a_p \in \mathbb N\text{,}\) only finitely many of which are non-zero.
