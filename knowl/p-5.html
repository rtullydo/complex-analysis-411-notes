This is pretty straighforward to see via the chain rule. Let \(\gamma(t)\) be a (differentiable) path contained in \(A\) with \(\gamma(0) = z_0\text{.}\) Let \(\sigma(t)\) be the image of \(\gamma\) under \(f\) defined by \(\sigma(t) = f(\gamma(t))\text{.}\) Then the chain rule gives

\begin{equation*} \sigma\dd(t) = f\dd(\gamma(t)) \cdot \gamma\dd(t) \end{equation*}

which in turn gives

\begin{equation*} \sigma\dd(0) = f\dd(z_0) \cdot \gamma\dd(0). \end{equation*}

That is, if \(f\dd(z_0) = r e^{i \theta}\) the image of the derivative of any curve \(\gamma\) through \(z_0\) is rotated by \(\theta\) and stretched by \(r\text{.}\)
