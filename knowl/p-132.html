Mathematicians of the 18th century, in the course of working out the basics of the theory of integral transforms, noticed that something special happened to the integral
\begin{equation*} \int_0^\infty f(t) e^{-t}\, dt \end{equation*}
when \(f(t) = t^n\text{.}\)
The astute reader may notice that this is very similar to the Laplace transform \(\mathcal{L}{f} = \int_0^\infty f(t) e^{-st} \, dt\text{.}\)
