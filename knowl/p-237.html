All of the pieces are set up. We now need Newman's Analytic Theorem (which we defer the proof of until later).

Lemma 3.3.7. Analytic Theorem.

Let \(f(t)\) be bounded and locally integrable for \(t \geq 0\text{,}\) and assume that
\begin{equation*} g(s) = \int_0^\infty e^{-st}f(t)\, dt \text{ with } \RE s > 0 \end{equation*}
extends to a holomorphic function on \(\RE s \geq 0\text{.}\) Then
\begin{equation*} \int_0^\infty f(t)\, dt \text{ converges} \end{equation*}
and equals \(g(0)\text{.}\)
Notice that \(f\) and \(g\) as we have defined them satisfy the hypotheses of the lemma, and so
\begin{equation*} \int_1^\infty \frac{\vtheta(x) - x}{x^2} \, dx = \int_0^\infty f(t) \, dt \text{ converges}, \end{equation*}
which establishes the theorem.
