Theorem 3.2.11. Product formula for \(\zeta\text{.}\).

For \(s \in \C\) with \(\RE s > 1\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation*} \zeta(s) = \prod_{p} (1 - \frac{1}{p^s})\inv, \end{equation*}

where the product is taken over the primes \(p\text{.}\) This representation is absolutely convergent.
