Certain functions behave like little rigid rotations locally.

Definition 1.1.1.

A function \(f: A \to B\) is called conformal if for each \(z_0 \in A\text{,}\) \(f\) rotates tangent vectors to curves passing through \(z_0\) by a fixed angle \(\theta\) and stretches tangent vectors to curves by a fixed factor \(r\text{.}\)
