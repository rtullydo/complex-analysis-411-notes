Proposition 1.6.3.

Let

\begin{equation*} T(z) = \frac{az + b}{cz + d}. \end{equation*}

Then \(T\) is bijective and conformal from \(A = \{z: cz + d \neq 0\}\) to \(B = \{w: cw - a\neq 0\}\text{.}\)

The inverse function \(T\inv:B \to A\) is also a linear fractional tranformation with formula

\begin{equation*} T\inv(w) = \frac{-dw + b}{cw - a}. \end{equation*}
