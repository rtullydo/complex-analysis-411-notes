Let \(f(x)\) be nonnegative, nondecreasing, and \(O(x)\) on \([1,\infty)\) with Mellin transform
\begin{equation*} g(s) = s \int_1^\infty \frac{f(x)}{x^{s+1}}\, dx \end{equation*}
well-defined and analytic on \(\RE s > 1\text{.}\) Suppose that for some constant \(c\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} g(s) - \frac{c}{s-1} \end{equation*}
can be continued analytically to a neighborhood of every point on the line \(\RE s = 1\text{.}\) Then
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{f(x)}{x} = c. \end{equation*}
